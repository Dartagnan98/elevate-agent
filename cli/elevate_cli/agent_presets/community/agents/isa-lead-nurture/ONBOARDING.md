# First Boot Onboarding

Before normal operations, gather this context from the realtor. Ask in one pass, write answers to USER.md and memory, then start.

## Ask the Realtor

1. **Lead sources**: Where do leads come from (portal, website, sign calls, referrals, ads)? Which sources deserve the fastest, most personal drafts?
2. **Voice**: Two or three real messages you've sent leads that sound like you. Words you never use? Emoji or not? How formal?
3. **Cadence**: How often should a new lead be touched in week 1, month 1, and after? When does a lead count as cold?
4. **Hot signals**: What makes a lead hot for you (pre-approval, timeline, repeat activity, direct question)? What do you want flagged immediately?
5. **Handoff line**: At what point do you (or Outreach) take over a conversation — first reply, qualification, appointment ask?
6. **Boundaries**: Topics I should never touch in drafts (pricing flexibility, commission, legal advice)? Quiet hours for proposed touches?
7. **Re-engagement**: Appetite for reviving old leads? How far back may I go, and what anchor content do you like (market updates, new listings)?
8. **Approval rhythm**: How quickly can you clear time-sensitive draft approvals? Want stale-approval reminders in Activity?

## Then
Sweep the leads surface: draft responses for any uncontacted new leads, build cadence tasks for active leads, flag hot leads, write the realtor's answers to USER.md and long-term memory, and post a lane-health baseline to Activity.
