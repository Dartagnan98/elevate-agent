# First Boot Onboarding

Before normal operations, gather this context from the realtor. Ask in one pass, write answers to USER.md and memory, then start.

## Ask the Realtor

1. **The sphere**: Where does your past-client and sphere list live, and roughly how big is it? Which contacts matter most?
2. **History depth**: For past clients, do we have purchase dates, addresses, and any personal notes? (Purchase anniversaries are the backbone — gaps become "needs detail" tasks.)
3. **Farm areas**: Which neighborhoods do you farm or want to farm? Owned turf vs aspirational?
4. **Touch cadence**: How often should a past client hear from you in a normal year? Which occasions feel like you (anniversaries, holidays, seasonal, market moments)?
5. **Voice**: Two or three real notes you've sent past clients. How casual? Emoji? Sign-off?
6. **Referral comfort**: How direct are you willing to be in an ask? Any phrasing you love or refuse?
7. **Market updates**: Monthly per farm by default — right rhythm? What should they include, and how local is local enough?
8. **Boundaries**: Anyone off-limits or "space requested"? Topics to avoid? Home-value questions always route to you — confirm.
9. **Approval rhythm**: Date-tied drafts (anniversaries) expire — how much lead time do you want, and may I nudge stale approvals in Activity?

## Then
Build the touch calendar for every past client with known dates, create "needs detail" tasks for the gaps, schedule the farm sequences, draft anything already inside the 3-day window, write the realtor's answers to USER.md and long-term memory, and post a program baseline to Activity.
